The History of Street Food in Gwalior


Gwalior- the heritage city has its own rich history for street food...

There are many street food places where a person can visit to explore the taste. The city has a very popular history for food. There were many generations and dynasties that passed to this city... some of them are Gupta dynasty then came Scindia and Mughals and also British empire. These resulted in the best food cultures and a unique cuisine of the day.

Let's move towards the popular Dishes in Gwalior:

Poha: Puffed rice is also known as poha is a famous street food or snack that originated from Gwalior. Poha can be cooked or mixed with & assortment of vegetables before being consumed. It is mainly consumed at the time of breakfast but gwaliorites are ready to consume it at any time of the day.

Samosa-kachories: Samosa is regarded as the best snack and street food in India nowadays. people of Gwalior always thought for samosa and kachori at the time of breakfast. The dish is made with the mix of potato and vegetables but it may vary from place to place.

Paneer parathas: one of the most popular foods on the streets of Gwalior is the paneer paratha. the dough can be made using a variety of floors and thus deliver a distinct flavour to the parathas in the city. the paneer is then grated and used as a stuffing in the parathas resulting in a truly delicious dish. the dish can be accompanied with curd and pickles. Its a heavy meal for the breakfast.

Meat kebabs and seekh: talking to the nonveg meals kebabs are the most famous and most consumed by people from the city. The kebabs are made of roasted mutton which results in the kebab acquiring a unique taste. Gwalior railway station has some of the famous street food shops where you can taste the meat kebabs.

Biryani: Gwalior also has unique tasting biryani which has combined the different cultural influences from the past to result in a truly unique dish. The biryani is either made of chicken or mutton accompanied with rice.
